Convexity

Math fundamentals note for ML preparation.

Core Idea

  • Convex objectives have no spurious local minima.

Key Condition

f(θx+(1−θ)y)≤θf(x)+(1−θ)f(y),  θ∈[0,1]f(\theta x + (1-\theta)y) \le \theta f(x)+(1-\theta)f(y),\;\theta\in[0,1]f(θx+(1−θ)y)≤θf(x)+(1−θ)f(y),θ∈[0,1]

Practical Notes

Convex models are easier to optimize reliably.

  • Logistic regression has convex loss; deep nets generally do not.

Regularization can improve effective optimization landscape.

  • Often helps numerical stability and generalization.

Why This Matters for ML

  • Convex objectives provide stronger guarantees for reaching globally optimal solutions.
  • Many classical models (linear/logistic with proper regularization) are optimized in convex settings.
  • Non-convexity in deep learning explains dependence on initialization and optimizer heuristics.
  • Convexity intuition helps compare algorithmic reliability across model families.
