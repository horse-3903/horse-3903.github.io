Step 5: Guessy Steganography

little did I know the sorts of atrocities this snake would commit

  • Arriving at this picture, test it by uploading it to https://aperisolve.com, but you’ll find nothing

  • An Yi also mentioned about how the top rows looked funny, reminding me of QR Codes and AI Image Upscalers, but it was just “regular steganography”.

  • According to Xi, brute forcing is possible (?), but seeing as how the solution space is 224=16,777,2162^{24} = 16,777,216224=16,777,216 combinations of bit planes / channels, I was stumped

use https://georgeom.net/StegOnline/upload

RBRGRRGBG, where every row has one r g or b with one row in exception of 2

have fun gng

  • Based on the hint from the An Yi and Xi, deduce that the bitplane combination is R7,R5,R4,R3,G5,G2,G0,B6,B1 or R7,B6,R5,G5,R4,R3,G2,B1,G0 (solution space reduced from 2242^{24}224 to 444 hooray!)
  • Alternatively, brute force the combinations of bit levels and bit channels with a heuristic English language checker (see naive brute forcing script here)
  • You’ll receive a list of three poems from the extraction, and a part of the flag nev3r_ after it!

Step 7: Blog

  • Clicking on the link brings you to https://storage.googleapis.com/zikorablog/index.html

  • Looking at the suspiciously placed download button, inspect to see whats being hidden and find another two parts of the flag

  • m1s5ing_1n_ in the space between where the download button is and where it should be

  • 7he_ in the CSS

  • Downloading the images, you’ll see that you need a string of numbers to complete the string of numbers 030008020107__________

Coordinates to the Zhejiang Museum Wulin Branch

Reddit says that these 免死金牌 had 333 characters.

  • 333: The Chinese text is coordinates: 30.2773218, 120.1634463, pointing to a museum/object related to an ancient 免死金牌 / 丹书铁券 (“death-sentence escape pass”). The inscription has 333 characters.

Location of the Nile River with respect to the map orientation is West.

  • 4: The map location shown is West of the Nile, but since the image is upside down, the Nile is to the “west” from there.

A quick Google search reveals that there are 138 Pyramids in Egypt.

  • 138: The drawing means “pyramids”. The clue asks how many pyramids were “seen”, and the commonly cited count for Egyptian pyramids is 138.

Google AI tells us that there the author of 《本草纲目》 tried 365 kinds of herbs and plants while writing the book.

  • 365: The question references the Chinese herbal medicine book《本草纲目》in which the author Li Shizhen tested 365 kinds of herbs while compiling it.

  • The final ordering by senses is: Heard 333 → Touch 4 → See 138 → Taste 365

  • Combining it with the other numbers in the main blog and padding with zeroes, you get the Hex key 03000802010703030304010308030605

  • Inspecting again, you find the AES Key in the meta id in Base64 w6VMVC2Kcvp1+2JUjuzdzQ==

  • This is a AES-CBC Decryption, hop onto Cyberchef
  • Cyberchef detects it as a Word Document, download the output as a file to obtain the final part of the flag! f1r57_p|4c3}
