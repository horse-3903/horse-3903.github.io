Setup

Definition:

Symmetric 3×33\times 33×3 simultaneous-move game with full-support and support-restricted mixing

  • Players: Two players, Player 1 and Player 2.
  • Strategies: Each player chooses AAA, BBB, or CCC.

Rules

  • Start with a symmetric 3×33\times 33×3 payoff matrix; Players simultaneously choose pure strategies or mix over supports.
  • The player who makes the opponent indifferent over the relevant support reaches mixed equilibrium.
  • Players move simultaneously.
  • Diagonal outcomes give payoffs 111, 222, and 333 at (A,A)(A,A)(A,A), (B,B)(B,B)(B,B), and (C,C)(C,C)(C,C); The off-diagonal entries below are a compact formalisation consistent with the visible support-analysis results in the lecture notes.

  • pure equilibria:

Social Optimum

  • Total payoff at (A,A)(A,A)(A,A) is 222.
  • Total payoff at (B,B)(B,B)(B,B) is 444.
  • Total payoff at (C,C)(C,C)(C,C) is 666.
  • Every off-diagonal outcome gives total payoff 000 or 111.
  • Therefore (C,C)(C,C)(C,C) is the unique social optimum.

Insights

Insight:

  • The new matrix supports one symmetric equilibrium for every nonempty support subset of {A,B,C}\{A,B,C\}{A,B,C}.
  • Support restrictions change the indifference equations, so each two-action support generates a different mixed equilibrium.
  • The Pareto-dominant equilibrium (C,C)(C,C)(C,C) is also the unique social optimum.
