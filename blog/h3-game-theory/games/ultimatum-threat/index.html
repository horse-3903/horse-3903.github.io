Ultimatum Game with a Non-Credible Threat

Game theory analysis: Ultimatum Game with a Non-Credible Threat.

1 May 2026
  • Content map: SMU H3 Game Theory Map

Setup

Definition:

Ultimatum Game with a Non-Credible Threat

  • Players: Two players, Sender / proposer and Receiver / responder.
  • Strategies: Sender chooses an offer xxx to give the receiver from a pie of size 101010; Receiver chooses Accept or Reject after every possible offer; Threat strategy: reject any offer with x<5x<5x<5.

Rules

  • Start with a fixed pie and a threatened rejection rule; Players move sequentially: the sender offers xxx, then the receiver accepts or rejects.
  • The player who ignores non-credible off-path threats reaches the subgame-perfect outcome.
  • If the receiver accepts offer xxx, payoffs are (10−x,x)(10-x,x)(10−x,x); If the receiver rejects, payoffs are (0,0)(0,0)(0,0).
  • Payoffs are ordered as (Sender, Receiver).

Game Tree

Derivation (Best Response Analysis)

  • If x>0x>0x>0, the receiver strictly prefers Accept because x>0x>0x>0.
  • If x=0x=0x=0, the receiver is indifferent between Accept and Reject.
  • Rejecting 0<x<50<x<50<x<5 is therefore not sequentially rational.
  • If the sender believes the receiver rejects all x<5x<5x<5, the sender’s best accepted offer is x=5x=5x=5.

Derivation (Nash Equilibrium)

  • Strategy profile:
Sender offers 5,Receiver rejects x<5 and accepts x≥5\text{Sender offers }5,\qquad \text{Receiver rejects }x<5\text{ and accepts }x\geq 5Sender offers 5,Receiver rejects x<5 and accepts x≥5
  • Given the receiver’s strategy, offering less than 555 gives sender payoff 000.
  • Offering 555 gives sender payoff 555.
  • Offering more than 555 gives sender payoff below 555.
  • Thus offering 555 is a best response to the receiver’s threat strategy.
  • Given offer 555, the receiver accepts and receives 555, so the receiver does not gain by deviating on the equilibrium path.
  • Hence the profile is a Nash equilibrium.

Nash Equilibrium

Result:

The profile

(Offer 5, Reject x<5, Accept x≥5)(\text{Offer }5,\ \text{Reject }x<5,\ \text{Accept }x\geq 5)(Offer 5, Reject x<5, Accept x≥5)

is a Nash equilibrium, but it is not subgame perfect because rejecting 0<x<50<x<50<x<5 is not credible.

Insights

Insight:

  • A threat can support a Nash equilibrium without being credible.
  • SGPE removes the threat because the receiver would not carry it out after a positive offer below 555.
  • Strategic moves are needed to make such threats credible.
