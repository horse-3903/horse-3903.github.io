  • Entry is inefficient when the incumbent is low cost because the entrant’s net payoff is negative:

Insights

Insight:

  • Raising the low-cost type from 555 to 101010 changes the equilibrium set: pooling and semi-separating can now exist.
  • Pooling works when the prior probability of the low-cost incumbent is large enough to make entry unattractive after p=17.5p=17.5p=17.5.
  • Semi-separating requires two indifference conditions:
    • The entrant must be indifferent after p=17.5p=17.5p=17.5.
    • The high-cost incumbent must be indifferent between p=17.5p=17.5p=17.5 and p=20p=20p=20.
  • The posterior belief μ=14\mu=\frac{1}{4}μ=41​ is pinned down by the entrant’s indifference condition.
