Incomplete-Information Trading Game

Game theory analysis: Incomplete-Information Trading Game.

1 May 2026
  • Content map: SMU H3 Game Theory Map

Setup

Definition:

Incomplete-Information Trading Game

  • Players: Three players, Player 1, Player 2, and Nature, who independently draws wallet values.
  • Strategies:
    • Nature draws w1w_1w1​ for Player 1 and w2w_2w2​ for Player 2.
    • Values are independent and uniformly distributed on Ω={5,6,7,…,55}\Omega=\{5,6,7,\dots,55\}Ω={5,6,7,…,55}.
    • Player 1 observes w1w_1w1​ and Player 2 observes w2w_2w2​ only, but both players know Nature’s distribution.
    • Each player chooses Keep or Swap after observing their own wallet.

Rules

  • Start with each player privately observing an independently drawn wallet value; Players simultaneously choose Keep or Swap after observing their own wallet.
  • The player who swaps only when it is profitable under beliefs maximises expected value.
  • If both players choose Swap, they trade wallets; If at least one player chooses Keep, no trade occurs.
  • Payoff equals final wallet value.
  • A Nash equilibrium is a pair of strategies (σ1,σ2)(\sigma_1,\sigma_2)(σ1​,σ2​) such that both strategies are mutual best responses.

Derivation (Best Response Analysis)

Dominance argument

  • Use iterated deletion of weakly dominated strategies.

  • If w=55w=55w=55,

    • Keep gives payoff of exactly 555555.
    • Swap gives payoff of at most 555555.
    • Hence, Keep is never worse and sometimes better than Swap.

  • If w=54w=54w=54,

    • Keep gives payoff of exactly 545454.
    • Swap gives payoff of at most 545454.
    • Hence, Keep is never worse and sometimes better than Swap.

  • Continue iteratively down to w=6w=6w=6, resulting in the only non-unique action at w=5w=5w=5.

  • To verify σ2\sigma^2σ2 is a best response to σ2\sigma^2σ2:
    • If wi>5w_i>5wi​>5, then σ1\sigma^1σ1 and σ2\sigma^2σ2 give the same payoff because no trade occurs.
    • If wi=5w_i=5wi​=5, then under σ2\sigma^2σ2 trade occurs only when the opponent also has 555, so payoff is always 555.
    • If wi=5w_i=5wi​=5, then under σ1\sigma^1σ1 payoff is also 555.

Nash Equilibrium

Result:

The cutoff equilibrium is

(σ2,σ2),(\sigma^2,\sigma^2),(σ2,σ2),

where each player swaps only when w=5w=5w=5 and keeps otherwise.

The strategy pairs

(σ1,σ1),(σ1,σ2),(σ2,σ1),(σ2,σ2)(\sigma^1,\sigma^1),\qquad (\sigma^1,\sigma^2),\qquad (\sigma^2,\sigma^1),\qquad (\sigma^2,\sigma^2)(σ1,σ1),(σ1,σ2),(σ2,σ1),(σ2,σ2)

Social Optimum

  • Total payoff is always
w1+w2.w_1+w_2.w1​+w2​.
  • Swapping only redistributes wallet values between players.
  • Ex post, both players weakly prefer swapping only when w1=w2w_1=w_2w1​=w2​.
  • There is no utilitarian surplus gain from trade.

Insights

Insight:

  • Willingness to swap signals a low wallet value.
  • Higher types refuse to trade because the opponent’s willingness to trade is bad news.
  • Trade occurs only at the lowest possible type.
  • This is a classic adverse-selection or lemons-market outcome.
