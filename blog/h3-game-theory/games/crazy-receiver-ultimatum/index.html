Setup

Definition:

Incomplete-Information Ultimatum Game

  • Players: Three players, Sender, Receiver, and Nature, who selects the receiver’s type.
  • Strategies: Sender chooses whether to offer 000 or 555 from a pie of size 101010; Rational receiver accepts any non-negative offer; Crazy receiver accepts only offers of at least 555.

Rules

  • Start with Nature selecting the receiver’s type with probability ppp; Players move sequentially: the sender offers 000 or 555, then the receiver accepts or rejects.
  • The player who chooses optimally given beliefs maximises expected payoff.
  • With probability ppp, the receiver is crazy.
  • With probability 1−p1-p1−p, the receiver is rational; The sender does not observe the receiver’s type.
  • Payoffs are ordered as (Sender, Receiver).

Derivation (Best Response Analysis)

  • If Sender offers 000, only the rational receiver accepts.
  • Sender’s expected payoff from offering 000 is:
10(1−p)10(1-p)10(1−p)
  • If Sender offers 555, all receivers accept.
  • Sender’s expected payoff from offering 555 is:
555

Derivation (Nash Equilibrium)

  • Offering 555 is optimal when:
5>10(1−p)5>10(1-p)5>10(1−p)
  • Therefore:
p>12p>\frac{1}{2}p>21​
  • If p>12p>\frac{1}{2}p>21​, the sender offers 555.
  • If p<12p<\frac{1}{2}p<21​, the sender offers 000.
  • If p=12p=\frac{1}{2}p=21​, the sender is indifferent.

Nash Equilibrium

Result:

Offering 555 is a Perfect Bayesian Equilibrium strategy if and only if:

p>12p>\frac{1}{2}p>21​

Insights

Insight:

  • A reputation for irrational toughness can shift bargaining power.
  • The receiver benefits when the sender assigns enough probability to the crazy type.
  • Generating sufficient doubt about rationality can be a strategic move.
