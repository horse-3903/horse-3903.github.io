Setup

Definition:

Finite Perfect-Information Game

  • Players: Player 1 and Player 2.
  • Strategies: Player 1 chooses L or R; Player 2 chooses u or d at the reached node.
  • Rules: Player 2 observes Player 1’s move, and optimal play is found by backward induction.

Game Tree

Payoff Details

  • (L,u)↦(2,0)(L,u) \mapsto (2,0)(L,u)↦(2,0)
  • (L,d)↦(0,2)(L,d) \mapsto (0,2)(L,d)↦(0,2)
  • (R,u)↦(1,4)(R,u) \mapsto (1,4)(R,u)↦(1,4)
  • (R,d)↦(2,3)(R,d) \mapsto (2,3)(R,d)↦(2,3)

Derivation (Backward Induction)

  • After LLL, Player 2 compares the second payoff from uuu and ddd: 000 vs 222. Hence Player 2 chooses ddd after LLL.
  • After RRR, Player 2 compares the second payoff from uuu and ddd: 444 vs 333. Hence Player 2 chooses uuu after RRR.

  • Anticipating Player 2’s optimal continuation actions, Player 1 compares the first payoff from LLL and RRR: 000 vs 111.
  • Hence Player 1 chooses RRR.

Nash Equilibrium

Result:

A subgame perfect Nash equilibrium strategy profile is:

{N1,(N2,N3)}↦{R,(d,u)}\{ N1, (N2, N3) \} \mapsto \{ R, (d, u) \}{N1,(N2,N3)}↦{R,(d,u)}

Insights

Insight:

In sequential games, strategies must specify actions at every contingency.

Backward induction selects optimal actions in every subgame.
