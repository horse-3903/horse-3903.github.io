About

I study how intelligent adaptive agents - whether reinforcement-learning models or market participants - produce systemic outcomes no individual agent intended. The Minsky Market Sim and the CAPTCHA-LLM paper are two instances of the same question: what emerges when adaptive systems encounter structural constraints?

Trained in AI and economics at Hwa Chong as a CSIT Computing Scholar, I bring quantitative methods to problems at the boundary of technology and institution. My research spans autonomous UAV obstacle detection (NTU, under Asst Prof Mir Feroskhan), computer vision for chemistry lab error detection (SSEF Finalist, 91.1% accuracy), and a first-author arXiv preprint on LLM perceptual limits in CAPTCHA security.

Outside research: Overall Captain of Hwa Chong Track & Field (250+ members), two-time National School Games C Division Champion in the 100m and 4x100m relay, and international sprinter for Singapore.

Publications

arXivApr 2026

Perceptual Gaps: ASCII Art and Overlapping Audio as CAPTCHA

Investigated the viability of human-specialised neural processing as a basis for next-generation CAPTCHAs resilient to multimodal LLMs. Introduced two CAPTCHA classes (ASCII art-based visual CAPTCHAs and noise-corrupted audio QA CAPTCHAs) and evaluated them against frontier models including GPT-4o and Gemini. No model solved a single ASCII-based CAPTCHA and all models performed near-randomly on audio CAPTCHAs, suggesting strong near-term robustness against current AI capabilities.

PythonLLMsCybersecurityResearch
Singapore Science and Engineering Fair (SSEF)Mar 2026

Enhancing Error Detection in Chemistry Experiments using Synthetic Data for Faster and More Robust Training

LabAssist: a computer vision system for detecting procedural errors in chemistry titration experiments. Investigated synthetic video data to improve model generalisation, comparing low-fidelity (background replacement, GMFlow optical flow) and high-fidelity (diffusion-based image animation) pipelines. GMFlow-based augmentation achieved 91.1% accuracy vs. an 83.3% real-world-only baseline, demonstrating that motion-preserving synthetic data generation is an effective strategy for scalable training dataset expansion.
